We study sequential decision-making when the agent's internal model class is misspecified. Within the infinite-horizon Berk-Nash framework, stable behavior arises as a fixed point: the agent acts optimally relative to a subjective model, while that model is statistically consistent with the long-run data endogenously generated by the policy itself.
